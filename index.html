Rizzato Lab

Low Dimensional Quantum Spin Platforms

The Rizzato Lab develops solid state spin defects in low dimensional materials as quantum platforms for spin resonance, nanoscale NMR, and quantum sensing.

Our research combines magnetic resonance, quantum control, and low dimensional materials physics to enable nanoscale spectroscopy in complex physical, chemical, and biological environments.

Research

Spin defects in low dimensional materials

Discovery and characterization of novel spin defects in two dimensional materials and nanotubes, focusing on coherence, control, and interaction with the local environment.

Quantum spin resonance and nanoscale NMR

Development of quantum spin resonance techniques that extend EPR and NMR into the nanoscale regime, using individual and few spin systems as local probes.

Quantum sensing and hyperpolarization

Quantum sensing protocols and spin polarization transfer schemes to enhance magnetic resonance sensitivity and enable new forms of nanoscale and in cell spectroscopy.
