The Rizzato Lab develops solid state spin defects in low dimensional materials as quantum platforms for spin resonance, nanoscale NMR, and quantum sensing.

Our research combines magnetic resonance, quantum control, and low dimensional materials physics to enable nanoscale spectroscopy in complex physical, chemical, and biological environments.

Research

Spin defects in low dimensional materials

We discover and characterize novel electronic spin defects in two dimensional materials and nanotubes, with particular focus on hexagonal boron nitride and boron nitride nanotubes. Our work explores coherence, quantum control, and coupling to the local environment at the atomic scale.

Quantum spin resonance and nanoscale NMR

We develop quantum spin resonance techniques that extend conventional EPR and NMR into the nanoscale regime. Individual and few-spin systems are used as local probes to access magnetic resonance signals from nanoscopic and heterogeneous systems.

Quantum sensing and hyperpolarization

We design quantum sensing protocols and spin polarization transfer schemes to enhance magnetic resonance sensitivity. Our goal is to enable nanoscale and in-cell spectroscopy under ambient conditions and at room temperature.
