The Rizzato Lab develops solid state spin defects in low dimensional materials as quantum platforms for spin resonance, nanoscale NMR, and quantum sensing.

We combine magnetic resonance, quantum control, and materials physics to enable nanoscale spectroscopy in complex environments.

Research

Spin defects in low dimensional materials

Discovery and characterization of novel spin defects in 2D materials and nanotubes, focusing on coherence, control, and coupling to the local environment.

Quantum spin resonance and nanoscale NMR

Quantum control methods that push EPR and NMR into the nanoscale regime using individual and few spin systems as local probes.

Quantum sensing and hyperpolarization

Sensing protocols and polarization transfer schemes to enhance magnetic resonance signals, aiming toward new routes for nanoscale and in cell spectroscopy.
